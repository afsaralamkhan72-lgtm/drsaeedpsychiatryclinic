About Dr. Muhammad Saeed Khan

Quick answer: Dr. Muhammad Saeed Khan is Chairman of Psychiatry at Hayatabad Medical Complex, FCPS-qualified, PMDC-registered, and CBT-certified. Learn about his qualifications, experience and approach to care.

Background & Current Role

Dr. Muhammad Saeed Khan is a consultant psychiatrist based in Peshawar, currently serving as Chairman of the Psychiatry Department at Hayatabad Medical Complex (HMC) - one of the leading tertiary care hospitals in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. Alongside his clinical practice, he is Assistant Professor at Khyber Girls Medical College (KGMC), where he teaches MBBS students, contributing to the training of the next generation of doctors in Peshawar.

Clinical Approach

Dr. Khan's practice is built around structured psychiatric assessment, clear communication with patients and families, and treatment plans tailored to each individual's history and circumstances - rather than a one-size-fits-all approach. He places particular emphasis on Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) as part of treatment where clinically appropriate, alongside psychiatric evaluation and, when indicated, medication management.

Every consultation - whether in person or online - is conducted with strict confidentiality and respect for patient dignity, in line with professional medical ethics and PMDC guidelines.

Conditions Treated

Dr. Khan provides assessment and treatment for a wide range of psychiatric conditions, including depression, anxiety disorders, OCD, PTSD, bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, ADHD, insomnia, addiction, child and adolescent psychiatry, and more. See the full list of conditions treated.

Where to Consult

Dr. Khan sees patients at Hayatabad Medical Complex, Phase IV, Peshawar (private practice, Monday-Friday 3:00 PM-8:00 PM) and at the Government Psychiatry Department, HMC (Monday-Friday 9:00 AM-1:00 PM). On weekends, he is available at the Dir Timergara clinic, opposite DHQ Hospital. Online video consultations are available for patients anywhere in Pakistan. Consultations are conducted in Urdu, Pashto and English.
